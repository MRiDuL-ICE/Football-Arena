Mesut Ozil

Mauro Icardi

Mauro Icardi is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a striker for Galatasaray on loan from Paris Saint-Germain. Icardi began his career with Argentine club Juventud Unida before moving to Europe, joining Italian clubs Sampdoria and Inter Milan. At Inter, Icardi established himself as one of the most prolific goal scorers in Serie A, winning the Capocannoniere (top scorer) award in 2015 and 2018.

Kylian Mbappé

Kylian Mbappé is a French professional footballer who plays as a forward for Ligue 1 club Paris Saint-Germain and the French national team. Considered one of the best players in the world, Mbappé burst onto the scene as a teenager with AS Monaco, winning the Ligue 1 title in 2017. He then transferred to Paris Saint-Germain, where he has continued to excel, winning multiple league titles and the 2018 FIFA World Cup with France.

Mohamed Salah

Mohamed Salah is an Egyptian professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Liverpool. Harry Kane

Harry Kane is an English professional footballer who plays as a striker for Premier League club Tottenham Hotspur and captains the English national team. Kane came through the youth academy at Tottenham and has spent his entire professional career with the club. He has established himself as one of the best strikers in world football, known for his exceptional goal-scoring ability, hold-up play, and link-up skills.

Kevin De Bruyne

Kevin De Bruyne is a Belgian professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Premier League club Manchester City. De Bruyne began his career in Belgium with Genk before moving to the Bundesliga with Werder Bremen and VfL Wolfsburg. It was at Wolfsburg where he truly blossomed into a world-class player, earning a big-money move to Manchester City in 2015.

Philippe Coutinho

Philippe Coutinho is a Brazilian professional footballer who plays as an attacking midfielder for Aston Villa on loan from Barcelona. Coutinho began his career in Brazil with Vasco da Gama before moving to Europe, joining Inter Milan and then Liverpool in 2013. At Liverpool, Coutinho established himself as one of the best playmakers in the Premier League, known for his dribbling, vision, and long-range shooting.
